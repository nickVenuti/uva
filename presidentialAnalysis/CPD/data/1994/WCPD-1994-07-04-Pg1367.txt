Memorandum on Assistance to South Africa

June 27, 1994

Presidential Determination No. 94-29

Memorandum for the Secretary of State

Subject: Eligibility of South Africa to be Furnished Defense Articles 
and Services Under the Foreign Assistance Act and the Arms Export 
Control Act

    Pursuant to the authority vested in me by section 503(a) of the 
Foreign Assistance Act of 1961, as amended, and section 3(a)(1) of the 
Arms Export Control Act, I hereby find that the furnishing of defense 
articles and services to the Government of South Africa will strengthen 
the security of the United States and promote world peace.
    You are authorized and directed to report this finding to the 
Congress and to publish it in the Federal Register.
                                            William J. Clinton
