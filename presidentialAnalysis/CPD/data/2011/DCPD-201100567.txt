Administration of Barack Obama, 2011 Message on Continuation of Emergency Regarding Export Control Regulations August 12, 2011 To the Congress of the United States: Section 202(d) of the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1622(d)) provides for the automatic termination of a national emergency unless, prior to the anniversary date of its declaration, the President publishes in the Federal Register and transmits to the Congress a notice stating that the emergency is to continue in effect beyond the anniversary date. In accordance with this provision, I have sent to the Federal Register for publication the enclosed notice, stating that the emergency caused by the lapse of the Export Administration Act of 1979, as amended, is to continue in effect for 1 year beyond August 17, 2011. BARACK OBAMA The White House, August 12, 2011. Categories: Communications to Congress : Export control regulations, U.S. national emergency, message on continuation.
