Message to the Congress on Continuation of the National Emergency 
Regarding Export Control Regulations

July 23, 2008

To the Congress of the United States:

    Section 202(d) of the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1622(d)), 
provides for the automatic termination of a national emergency unless, 
prior to the anniversary date of its declaration, the President 
publishes in the Federal Register and transmits to the

[[Page 1019]]

Congress a notice stating that the emergency is to continue in effect 
beyond the anniversary date. In accordance with this provision, I have 
sent to the Federal Register for publication the enclosed notice, 
stating that the emergency caused by the lapse of the Export 
Administration Act of 1979, as amended, is to continue in effect for 1 
year beyond August 17, 2008.
                                                George W. Bush
 The White House,
 July 23, 2008.

Note: This message was released by the Office of the Press Secretary on 
July 24.
